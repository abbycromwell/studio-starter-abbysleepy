# Week 3 prompts — case study + motion

Main task this week: finish the site. Every page in your navigation gets real
content, with one project told well as the centerpiece, and your first motion.

Case studies worth studying before you start:
[Climatescope](https://viviancromwell.com/product-design/climatescope) and
[Reverie](https://viviancromwell.com/product-design/reverie) — the second one
was built with the same AI workflow you're using.

Before prompting any motion, spend ten minutes in the
[motion vocabulary](https://viviancromwell.com/vocabularybyemil) — an interactive
glossary with live demos. You can only prompt what you can name: "stagger the
cards' entrance, ease-out, 40ms delays" beats "make it smoother" every time.

## In session — the case study

1. **Draft from your notes**
   > Here are my notes and images for "[project name]": [paste notes]. Draft it into the case-study template in src/pages/work/ — role, context, constraints, decisions, iteration, outcome — and rename the page after the project. Keep my words wherever they're good.

2. **Make it yours** — the rewrite is the human part:
   > Read the case study back to me one section at a time. I'll tell you what to change — keep my phrasing when I dictate it, don't smooth it out.

3. **First motion**
   > /impeccable animate the case study page

4. **Why this animation?** — make the AI defend every choice:
   > Walk me through every animation you just added: what it communicates, why that easing and duration, and what breaks if we remove it. Then check each one against the emil-design-engineering animation checklist — easing choice, duration, paired elements, the frequency rule. Remove anything that fails.

## Through the week — finish everything

5. **Hunt down the placeholders**
   > Go through every page in my navigation. List anything that's still placeholder or thin, then work through the list with me until every page is real.

6. **One memorable interaction**
   > Read the motion personality in DESIGN.md. Propose three options for one memorable interaction on my site — small, tasteful, mine. Build the one I pick.

7. **Get creative (stretch goal)** — make one or two interactivity samples and
   add them to your portfolio. Four tools, listed gentlest to most technical.
   Pick ONE:
   - [Jitter](https://jitter.video) — polished motion graphics in minutes, no code; gentlest on-ramp
   - [Spline](https://spline.design) — real-time 3D scenes visitors can orbit and hover; visual, but 3D thinking takes a minute
   - [Rive](https://rive.app) — graphics with logic that respond to cursor and clicks; the state machine is a new way of thinking
   - [Motion.dev](https://motion.dev) — animation as code; most technical, but Claude and Codex write it with you

   Then:
   > I picked [Spline / Motion.dev / Rive / Jitter] and made [describe what you made or want]. Help me add it to my site — where it should live, how to embed it, and how to keep it tasteful per DESIGN.md.

By Friday: no placeholders anywhere, one case study in your own voice, motion
that means something, and a running list of app ideas for Project 2. Stretch
goal: one or two interactivity samples live in your portfolio.
