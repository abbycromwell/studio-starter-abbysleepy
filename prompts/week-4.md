# Week 4 prompts — polish + launch

The point of this week is not a finished website. It's a live website plus the
loop you'll run forever: critique, fix, ship. Feedback doesn't stop when the
week does.

## In session — the experiment pass

1. **Baseline first** — know where you stand:
   > /impeccable critique my site

2. **The experiment loop** — repeat with `layout` and `colorize`:
   > Commit what we have so we can revert. Then run /impeccable typeset on my homepage and show me what changed and why. I'll keep it or ask you to revert.

   The ritual matters more than any single result: commit, experiment, judge,
   keep or revert. Git makes taste experiments free.

## Through the week — gates, findability, launch

3. **The technical gates**
   > /impeccable audit my site — then turn the findings into a fix list, ordered by impact, and work through it with me.

4. **Contrast and accessibility**
   > Run the rams skill on my components. Fix the contrast and accessibility findings — explain each fix in one line.

5. **The mobile pass** — then open it on your real phone:
   > /impeccable adapt my site for mobile

6. **Findable and shareable**
   > Give every page a proper title and description, add social-preview tags, and show me what my link will look like when shared. Then run the favicon skill on [your image].

7. **Ship it**
   > Run the polish skill on my changes, commit, push, and deploy. Then give me the final URL and a one-paragraph summary of what shipped in four weeks.

By Friday: a portfolio that passes the gates, refined by experiments you chose
to keep, findable when shared — live at a URL you're proud to text someone.
Stretch goal continues: keep growing your interactivity samples. And keep the
loop running: critique, fix, ship.
